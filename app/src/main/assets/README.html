About CTU Clock

What is CTU?

CTU (Calculated Time Uncoordinated) is a timekeeping system designed to align with the natural solar day.

  • 12:00 CTU is always aligned with your local solar noon.
  • 00:00 CTU is always aligned with your local solar midnight.
  • The minor daily variations in the solar day are absorbed by dynamically adjusting the length of the final hour (23:00 to 00:00 CTU). All other hours are standard 60 minutes.

Why Use CTU?

  • No Daylight Saving Time: CTU eliminates the need for disruptive clock changes by staying anchored to the sun.
  • Circadian Alignment: By reflecting the true solar day, CTU helps you intuitively align with your body's natural sleep-wake cycle.
  • Universal Solar Context: "15:00 CTU" gives you a sense of the sun's position relative to noon, no matter where you are on Earth.

Using the App

  • Time Modes: The clock displays three time types:
    • Global (UTC): Standard Coordinated Universal Time.
    • Regional: Your device's current time zone.
    • Local (CTU): The calculated CTU time for your current location.
  • Switching Modes: Swipe left or right on the clock display to cycle through the UTC, Regional, and CTU time modes.
  • Date Display: Below the main time, you'll see the Weekday and ISO Week number (e.g., "We 34"). Tap this label to toggle the display to the full date (YYYY-MM-DD). It will automatically switch back after a few seconds.
  • Sun Animation (CTU Mode): In CTU mode, a small sun icon animates along an ellipse, visually representing the sun's position relative to solar noon (top of the ellipse at 12:00 CTU) and solar midnight (bottom at 00:00 CTU).
  • Dawn/Dusk Times (CTU Mode): When in CTU mode, the calculated dawn (↑) and dusk (↓) times for your location are shown at the bottom, providing additional solar context. The sun icon changes color around these times.
